Hello, world

My career began in 2015, writing static websites with HTML, CSS, and vanilla Javascript—though I think it was simply called Javascript back then.

It was awesome: SFTP'ing into the server, editing files in TextEdit, and seeing changes instantly appear on a live website anyone could visit. My mom received tons of links— "look what I'm working on!", always followed by the instruction to refresh and "look now!".

The way we build for the web has certainly changed a lot since then. For many projects now, there are more layers to consider and more interacting parts to manage than in those early days. Keeping up has meant my own approach and toolkit have had to evolve too.

Now, my wife gets links.

I'm still excited by everything I work on: every problem I get to solve with code and, yes, every time I get to be wrong—that's a true blessing. There's a ton I still don't know, but I've always enjoyed finding out.

— Ryan Dawes, 2025
